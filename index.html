A little about myself

Right now, you're probably wondering how I got to this point. Allow me to rewind it back for you a little bit...

In 2005, I graduated from Indiana University of Kokomo with a Purdue degree (yes, you read that correctly) majoring in Computer Technology. During my years at the university, I learned about various items such as "HTML" and software development using "Visual Basic 6.0".

For a few years after graduating with my degree, I worked in retail before being brought onboard by an IT company. For the majority of my twelve year tenure at this company, I worked within their Telecom department, responsible for administrating the company's numerous telephone systems.

However, I was furloughed and eventually let go from the company due to the Covid-19 pandemic.

Deciding to take that negative and turn it into a positive, I took the opportunity to think about a career change, as working in the Telecom industry did not feel self-fulfilling to me.

After partaking in a free online introduction seminar to web development offered by Eleven Fifty Academy, I re-discovered the joy that I used to feel during the HTML course that I took back during my Computer Technology major over fifteen years prior.

I am now currently enrolled in a twelve-week long full-time course with Eleven Fifty Academy for web development. During my time so far, I have garnered knowledge in aspects such as HTML, CSS, Javascript, and TypeScript. I have been using React for my front-end development. I also have experience with Bootstrap, Reactstrap, Material UI, as well as several different CSS and animation online tools.

Using specific branding provided by the Spotify for Developers API: I created a project that utilizes full CRUD functionality that allows users to create accounts to build music playlists. This site was built using React Legacy (class components) and Material UI.

Using the official COVID-19 API, I created a project that fetches the most current data related to cases of COVID-19.

This project pulls both the new and total count of new cases, deaths, and recoveries of COVID-19 patients.

My very first project within our web development course at Eleven Fifty Academy was to create a creature using only CSS code. I went with the classic Nintendo character known as "Mega Man".

Using an endpoint provided by the public NASA API, I created a gallery that displays the five most recent NASA pictures of the day.

One of my earlier projects was to take an existing website and duplicate its visible layout to the best of our ability.
